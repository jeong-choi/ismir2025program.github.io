LP-23: Improvisation of tabla the indian percussion using fibre

Retnasree Iyer

Abstract: In the present research work an attempt was made to construct the body of the daya component of tabla with processed fibre. The scientific parameters were analysed and compared with the standard instrument. The tonal quality calibration of the instrument was digitally performed in a studio environment with the help of a computer and an interface. The analysis showed that there was difference in the single tone “tha” and “thi” in terms of the peak frequency. The maximum frequency of “tha” in wooden daya was 770 Hz whereas in fibre daya it was 526 Hz. The peak frequency of “thin” in wooden daya was 236 and that in fibre daya was 278Hz. Frequency analysis of combined tones also showed difference in the peak frequencies. Generally, it could be observed that the peak frequency difference was more in open notes than in closed notes.
